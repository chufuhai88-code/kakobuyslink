Agent basics: money in, boxes out, QC in between

Unless stated otherwise, wallets, warehouse time, photos, and airwaybills sit with your agent—not this static site. Think of the blocks below as rehearsal for what support tickets usually sound like. Dollars, days, and exclusions are always whatever Kakobuy shows you today.

1 · Recharging your agent balance

Agents such as Kakobuy typically accept several top-up paths (for example PayPal or cards—the exact menu changes over time).

  • Add funds before or right after submitting an order so domestic payment to the marketplace seller is not delayed.
  • Transfers may appear instantly or after a short review, depending on the method and verification.

2 · Requesting refunds of account balance

If you no longer plan to shop, unused balance is usually refundable through your account’s withdrawal or refund flow. Processing time and payout channel depend on the original payment rail—check Kakobuy’s current policy before relying on timing.

3 · Warehouse free storage vs paid storage

After your parcel arrives at the agent warehouse, standard free storage windows are limited (often measured in tens of days, but policies vary by agent and season).

  • Plan international shipping early to avoid overdue storage charges.
  • During sale peaks, warehouses can be fuller—combine shipments when sensible.

4 · Why tracking stalls after dispatch

International tracking gaps are common: handoffs between airlines, hubs, customs, or last-mile carriers can delay scans for several days.

Usually the parcel is moving even when the webpage has not refreshed—only escalate if tracking is dormant well beyond carrier norms or your chosen line’s SLA.

5 · Estimated vs actual parcel weight

Shipping quotes shown pre-packaging are estimates. Agents re-weigh boxed parcels; if you paid more than freight actually costs after packing, surplus shipping is commonly credited back—but policy wording belongs to the agent.

6 · Customs inspection

Importing carries inherent risk of spot checks or duty assessment. Factors include declared value, line choice, commodity type, and local rules.

  • Truthful customs declarations generally reduce downstream problems.
  • Research your country’s de minimis thresholds and prohibited categories.

7 · QC (quality control) photos & inspection

Before you approve international postage, warehouse photos validate color, stitching, branding alignment, sizing tags, accessories, etc.

  • Compare QC against listing photos before “green-lighting.”
  • Many exchanges or cancellations are feasible only prior to exporting from China—timing matters.

8 · Problems after receipt

If damage or major mismatch appears after doorstep delivery:

  • Open a ticket promptly with timestamps and photographic evidence.
  • Resolution hinges on courier insurance, carrier terms, seller cooperation, and your agent policy.

9 · Parcel insurance

For high resale value or fragile pieces, selectable insurance can blunt loss-in-transit or severe damage—but coverage caps vary. Read exclusions before ticking the box.

10 · Prohibited & sensitive goods

Replica policies, lithium batteries, liquids, perfumes, aerosols, plant matter, counterfeit risks, batteries in electronics—all may be barred or routed only through certain freight lines.

Treat marketplace listings labeled “risky” literally; spreadsheets curate URLs but cannot legalize shipments your country forbids.

11 · Declined payments or extra verification

Cross-border gateways sometimes flag large or back-to-back top-ups.

  • Retry another card, payment service, or a smaller single transaction amount.
  • Complete verification steps your payment provider asks for—the agent cannot bypass AML rules.

12 · Duties & import taxes

Recipients—not kakobuyslink—are legally responsible for duties, brokerage, or VAT equivalents when customs assess them. Budget VAT/DHL handling fees beside pure freight.

13 · Combining shipments & packaging cuts

Most agents consolidate multiple Taobao carts into fewer boxes, omit shoe boxes upon request to hit volumetric breakpoints, bubble-wrap electronics, strip outer retail cartons—all good levers IF you declare combined items accurately.

14 · Promo codes & referral credits

If Kakobuy or another agent distributes coupons during campaigns, stacking them thoughtfully with spreadsheet finds can shave service or shipping percentages—always mirror code terms at checkout rather than trusting forum screenshots.

Note: Static copy goes stale fast. When numbers on this page disagree with Kakobuy—or any agent you use—trust their live policy pages and ticket replies.
